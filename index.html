What is Fungal Nanotechnology?

  • Fungi act as biological factories to synthesize nanoparticles
  • Uses enzymes and metabolites to reduce metal ions
  • Produces silver, gold, platinum nanoparticles naturally
  • Green, eco-friendly alternative to chemical methods

How Synthesis Works

  • Intracellular: Metal reduction occurs inside fungal cells
  • Extracellular: Using cell-free filtrates or biomass
  • Control: Adjust temperature, pH, concentration for size tuning
  • Common fungi: Aspergillus, Fusarium, Penicillium

Challenges & Limitations

  • Scalability: Large-scale production remains challenging
  • Standardization: Difficulty in controlling particle size and shape consistency
  • Toxicity Concerns: Long-term effects need more research
  • Cost: Purification and characterization can be expensive
  • Time: Biological synthesis is slower than chemical methods
